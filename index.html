SSGesture: Audio-driven Semantic and Stylistic Speech Gesture Generation via Diffusion Models

Abstract

Automatic generation of gestures driven by speech is a hot topic in the field of computer graphics. However, current approaches often overlook a wide range of modalities and their interactions, resulting in gestures with low contextual variation and noticeable jitter. To address these issues, we propose a novel diffusion-based framework named SSGesture, designed to effectively capture cross-modal associations. We establish a three-layer attention structure corresponding to local, semantic, and global attention to better handle multimodal information. We first design methods to automatically resolve style conflicts to control the gesture style by interpolation, as well as implement a unified unmarked style prompt structure through the AdaIN layer. To strengthen stability and diversity, we further control the seed gesture to balance diversity and gesture quality. Unlike previous methods, the proposed method for generating 3D gestures is semantically strong, greatly reducing the issues of large movements and jitter in posture.
